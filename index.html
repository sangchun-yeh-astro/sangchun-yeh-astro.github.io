Home

Hello! Welcome to my page! My name is Sangchun Yeh, but I also go by Harry. I am currently a first year undergraduate student at Ecole Polytechnique double majoring in math and physics. I am an aspiring astrophysicist. Check out here to learn more about me.

I have previossly done summer research as a high school student at the University of Hong Kong and Seoul National University. You can check out my work here.

Outside academics, I enjoy classical music. I have been playing the violin for almost a decade and have founded the Shenzhen International Youth Orchestra where I served as the director and conductor.

Work

While an intern at the University of Hong Kong, I worked on the morphologies of plantary nebulae under the supervision of Dr. Chih-Hao Hsia. In particular, we were interested in investigating the relationship between the shape of planetary nebulae and the presence of a binary system as its central star. I have contributed to multiple studies on planetary nebulae, including Wen et al. (2024) and Wen et al. (2026) .

I also worked on gravitational wave astrophysics when I was a summer research intern at the Center of Gravitational Wave University, Seoul National University under the supervision of Dr. Elahe Khalouei and Prof Hyung Mok Lee. I evaluated the performance and suitability of various supervised machine learning models for identifying gravitational lensing effects in gravitational-wave signals. I have since developed a deep interest in this field and will be continuing research of similar topic under the supervision of Prof. Otto Hannuksela this summer at the Chinese University of Hong Kong.
